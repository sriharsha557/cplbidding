# CPL 2025 Auction Rules & Process

## Overview

The Colruyt Premier League (CPL) 2025 uses a **Category-Based Auction System** designed to ensure fair distribution of players across all teams. This system prevents any single team from dominating by acquiring all the best players in one category.

## Team Structure

### Pre-Assigned Players (Before Auction)

- **1 Captain** üëë
- **1 Vice-Captain** ü•à

### Squad Composition
- **Squad Size Range**: 11-16 players per team
- **Available Slots for Auction**: 9-14 players (plus 2 pre-assigned)
- **Minimum Squad**: 11 players (9 from auction + 2 pre-assigned)
- **Maximum Squad**: 16 players (14 from auction + 2 pre-assigned)

## Auction Process

### Phase-Based Auction

The auction is conducted in **4 sequential phases**, one for each player category:

#### **Phase 1: Batsmen** üèè
- All batsmen are auctioned first (31 available)
- Teams bid within their batsman budget (250-350 tokens)
- Must acquire 3-4 batsmen

#### **Phase 2: All-rounders** ‚≠ê
- All all-rounders are auctioned second (33 available)
- Teams bid within their all-rounder budget (200-350 tokens)
- Must acquire 3-5 all-rounders
- **Most flexible category** - can fill multiple roles

#### **Phase 3: Bowlers** ‚öæ
- All bowlers are auctioned third (21 available) ‚ö†Ô∏è **LIMITED**
- Teams bid within their bowler budget (200-300 tokens)
- Must acquire 2-3 bowlers
- **High competition expected** - only 21 for 8 teams!

#### **Phase 4: WicketKeepers** üß§
- All wicketkeepers are auctioned last (14 available) ‚ö†Ô∏è **LIMITED**
- Teams bid within their wicketkeeper budget (100-200 tokens)
- Must acquire 1-2 wicketkeepers
- **Critical phase** - every team needs at least one!

## How Bidding Works

### Base Price
- Each player has a **base price** (minimum bid amount)
- Bidding starts at the base price
- Teams can bid higher in increments of 5 tokens

### Live Auction Screen
The admin panel displays:
- **Current Player**: Photo, name, role, base price
- **Team Budgets**: Remaining tokens for each team
- **Category Budget**: Remaining budget for the current player's category
- **Affordability Check**: Real-time validation if a team can afford the bid

### Bidding Rules
1. **Auction Timer**: 60 seconds per player
2. **Open Bidding**: Teams call out their bids verbally
3. **Admin Records**: The auction admin enters the final winning bid
4. **Validation**: System automatically checks:
   - Does the team have enough total tokens?
   - Does the team have enough category budget?
   - Does the team have space in their squad?
   - Does the team have space for this role?

### Outcomes
- **Sold**: Player is assigned to the winning team
- **Unsold**: If no team bids, player is marked as unsold

## Fair Distribution Mechanism

### Why Category-Based Budgets?

**Problem Without Categories:**
- A rich team could spend 800 tokens on 5 star batsmen
- Other teams would be left with weaker batsmen
- Unfair competition

**Solution With Categories:**
- Maximum 400 tokens can be spent on batsmen
- Forces teams to distribute spending across all categories
- Ensures every team gets quality players in each role

### Example Scenario

**Team A's Budget Status:**
- Total Tokens Left: 800 ü™ô
- Batsman Budget Left: 50 ü™ô
- Bowler Budget Left: 350 ü™ô

**Current Player:** A star batsman with base price 40 tokens

**Can Team A bid 100 tokens?**
- ‚úÖ Total tokens available: 800 (enough)
- ‚ùå Batsman budget available: 50 (NOT enough)
- **Result:** Team A can only bid up to 50 tokens

This prevents Team A from overspending on batsmen and forces balanced spending.

## Strategy Tips

### For Team Managers

1. **Plan Your Budget**
   - Decide how much to spend on star players vs. budget players
   - Don't exhaust your category budget too early

2. **Balance Your Squad**
   - Aim for a mix of experienced and new players
   - Ensure you meet minimum requirements in each category

3. **Watch Competitors**
   - Track other teams' remaining budgets
   - Identify when teams are running low on category budgets

4. **Save for Key Players**
   - Don't spend all your budget in early phases
   - Keep tokens for critical roles (e.g., wicketkeepers are limited)

### For Players

1. **Understand Your Value**
   - Your base price reflects your skill level
   - Higher base price = higher demand

2. **Category Matters**
   - All-rounders and wicketkeepers are in high demand (fewer slots)
   - Batsmen and bowlers have more slots available

3. **Timing is Everything**
   - Being auctioned early vs. late in your category affects your price
   - Teams with more budget left tend to bid higher

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What happens if a team runs out of budget?
**A:** The team can no longer bid on players. They must rely on unsold players or forfeit remaining slots.

### Q: Can a team exceed the maximum squad size?
**A:** No. The system prevents any team from acquiring more than 15 players total.

### Q: What if no one bids on a player?
**A:** The player is marked as "Unsold" and may be re-auctioned later or remain unassigned.

### Q: Can teams trade players after the auction?
**A:** Trading rules will be announced separately. The auction system focuses on initial team formation.

### Q: How are base prices determined?
**A:** Base prices are set by the organizing committee based on player experience, past performance, and role demand.

### Q: Can a team skip bidding in a category?
**A:** No. Teams MUST meet the minimum player requirements in each category (e.g., at least 4 batsmen).
